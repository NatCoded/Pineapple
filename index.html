Pizza

Let's address the big one right away. Pineapple on pizza. For some reason this is a controversial topic. Peronally I think even arguing about it is boring and played out. If you don't like it, fine. That's your prerogative. People have different tastes and that is okay. But personally pineapple adds the most wonderful sweet tangy to help compliment the marinara and offset the cheese. Perhaps a controversial opinion but I actually think that Hawaiian is one of the least interesting ways to eat pizza. Yes ham and pineapple go together wonderfully (we'll get to that later) but as far as complimenting your pizza go that is just the tip of the iceberg. Pineapple and pepperoni are a far more interesting combination in my mind with the spiciness of the pepperoni bringing a strong balancing flavor to the mix. On a similar note, jalapeño also work well.

The delictable pineapple really brings something to pizza that I think few other toppings can. The next closest thing that I can think of would be the tomato, something that no one disputes as belonging on a pizza. Both vibrant acidic fruits that bring a slight sweet edge that perfects a savory classic.

Pork & Ham

Aren't these the same thing? Well yes, but we need to get some SEO on this site come on! How can I just pick one? I won't and neither should you. Pineapple works well with most, if not all meat. A good cut of beef or breast of of chicken are only enhanced by some pineapple. Scientifically it makes sense. The acidity of pineapple helps to tenderize the meat making it softer and more delicate as you enjoy it, in addition to the flavor it brings.

None, however top the matrimony of flavor that is pork and pineapple. Pork is often a salty meat and this saltiness can be overwehelming at times. The sour sweetness of pineapple is here to balance it though, are you noticing a pattern? If you've never partaken in a holiday ham enhanced by the fruit of pineapple you have not yet experienced the true joy of the seasons.
